Book Recommendations

The 7 Habits Of Highly Effective People

This is the book that introduced me to the wonders of productivity. In this bestseller, Stephen Covery outlines the most important principles that lead to being effective as a human being. It provides a great overview to anyone who wants start working on themseleves, and I highly recommend it to every person who is looking for a way to get into non-fiction books.

Deep Work

In Deep Work, Cal Newport introduces us to the detrimental effects of the distractions that saturate today's world. With the use of beautifully told anecdotes, the book armed me with many tactics to minimize the distractions, and make the most of the time I spend working on projects. If you're a teenager in the 21st century, this book is a must-read.

The Power Of Habit

"Habits maketh man" is something I say quite often. Well, not really, but it's very true. The Power Of Habit is the book to read if you're interested in forming new habits, or breaking old one. I've referenced to it's ideas many times in the blog, and for good reason. Along with the many useful ideas about habits, Charles Duhigga also tells many interesting stories, making it a great read.

A Mind For Numbers

I love this book. I urge every student in the world to reads this wonderful work of writing by Barbara Oakley. I learned an extraordinary amount of things from this book - memory tricks, how to procrastinate effectively, how to take a test properly and so much more. It made a difference too. After it, I was studying less while getting better grades. This book wil drastically change the way you study, and it'll defintely be for the better.

Spark

We all know exercise is important; the internet tells us regularly. However, in Spark, John Ratey helps you understand it's importance. It's importance not only for the body, but for the mind. I procrastinated starting this book for a long time, and boy, was I wrong. This book encouraged to become serious about exercise, and I'm sure it'll do the same for you too.

The Happiness Equation

While I don't believe in chasing happiness, I still think this book is essential. Productivity involves setting goals, and when you accomplish one goal, you immeidiately move to bigger one; a loop that causes certain despair. In this book, Neil Pasricha constructs a manual to help us get out of this loop, and make sure the pursuit of being productive doesn't interfere with our general happiness.
